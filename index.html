I'm from Honduras and I'm R-Shiny developer since almost three years ago.

I started my journey when I worked in home office with a Chilean company. Where I had to learn R to be able to work with national survey data. At first it was a little difficult because I had never used R, but little by little I learned.

After that I started learning Shiny. for to be able to propose different types of ways to display the data worked with R like tables, maps and different kind of charts. It was like this how I started to get interest on Shiny.

I know there are still many things to know but you never stop learning.

Currently I'm studying about R-Shiny and more tools and programing languages.
